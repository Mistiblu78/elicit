# Elicit — UI Disclaimer and Compliance Notice

## Primary Notice (displayed prominently on every session — above the input form)

---

**Your client data stays yours.**
Elicit holds all case information in your active session only. Nothing is stored, logged, sold, or used to train AI models. When your session ends, it's gone.

**You are the attorney of record.**
Every document Elicit drafts is a starting point, not a final work product. You must review, verify, and approve before filing or serving. AI-assisted drafting does not transfer professional responsibility.

**Verify before you serve.**
AI tools can produce plausible but incorrect output. Always confirm that requests are accurate, complete, and appropriate for your specific matter. Manually verify any rule citations or legal references.

*Elicit is designed to support compliance with the Texas Responsible AI Governance Act (TRAIGA), Texas Disciplinary Rules of Professional Conduct Rules 1.01 and 1.05, Texas Ethics Opinion 705, and ABA Formal Opinion 512.*

---

## Input Field Notice (displayed next to the case notes input field)

**Do not enter real names, Social Security numbers, account numbers, or other identifying information in the case notes field.** Use role-based labels instead: [PETITIONER], [RESPONDENT], [CHILD], [BUSINESS]. Your real party names, counsel, cause number, court, and attorney details are entered in the form fields above — the app inserts them into your document automatically. Nothing is saved to our servers at any point.

---

## On First Use / Onboarding Screen

**Before you begin — three things to know:**

1. **Two fields, two approaches.** Enter your real party names, opposing counsel, cause number, court, and attorney details in the identifying information fields — the app inserts them into your document automatically. In the case notes field, describe the facts using role labels ([PETITIONER], [RESPONDENT], [CHILD]) instead of real names. This keeps confidential client information out of the AI prompt, protecting confidentiality under Texas DRPC Rule 1.05 and Texas Ethics Opinion 705.

2. **Review every draft before serving.** Elicit drafts discovery requests based on the facts you provide. You are responsible for verifying accuracy, confirming interrogatory limits, and ensuring the requests fit your strategy.

3. **This tool does not store your data.** Your session is private and temporary. No case information is retained after your session ends, consistent with TRAIGA and zero-retention design principles.
